This year's Best Picture nominees lineup is the strongest in recent memory, ranging from a soul-reaching vampire musical film, to a character study of a self-mythologising hustler, to a spell-bindingly beautiful tribute to man's interaction with nature. The horror genre sees its greatest recognition in an awards season ever, with Sinners leading in the number of nominations, and multiple horror performances leading the race for the Best Supporting categories.

As always, the Oscars race is riddled with controversies, though none as drastic as [last year's race]({% post_url 2025-02-28-2025-oscars-predictions %}). What made the headlines this year was Best Actor frontrunner Timothée Chalamet's comments about ballet and opera being dying art forms that "no one cares about," which triggered an uproar, particularly among those communities. Whether you agree with his statement or not, there is, however, a parallel to be drawn for the movie industry, especially cinemas and theaters, which are at risk of the same fate that befalls once-popular art forms that end up becoming niche.
